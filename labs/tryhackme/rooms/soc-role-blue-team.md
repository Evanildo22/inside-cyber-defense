## Overview

This room explores the SOC analyst's position within the broader Blue Team function. It examines how defensive security operations fit into an organisation's security posture and how SOC analysts contribute to threat detection, prevention, and response.

---

## Key Topics Covered

- The Blue Team's role in organisational security
- How SOC fits within the wider security department
- Reactive vs proactive defensive security functions
- Threat detection and monitoring responsibilities
- The relationship between SOC analysts and incident responders
- Overview of key Blue Team tools: SIEM, IDS/IPS, EDR, threat intelligence platforms

---

## Key Takeaways

- Blue Team is not just reactive — proactive threat hunting and continuous monitoring are equally important
- SOC analysts are the first line of detection; their accuracy directly affects how quickly threats are contained
- Tooling alone doesn't build a strong SOC — process, playbooks, and communication are equally critical
- Understanding attacker methodology (Red Team thinking) makes Blue Team analysts significantly more effective
